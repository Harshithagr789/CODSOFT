Dr. B.R.Ambedekar

1891-1956

Babasaheb

Dr.B.R.Ambedkar,born on April 14,1891,was a prominent Indian jurist,social reformer,and the chief architect of the Indian Constitution.He dedicated his life to eradicating caste-based discrimination and promoting social justice.Ambedkar was born into the marginalized "untouchable" Dalit community and faced discrimination throughout his life. He pursued higher education and became one of the first Dalits to earn a doctorate.His extensive knowledge and commitment to social reform led to his involvement in the drafting of India's Constitution.He played a crucial role in ensuring fundamental rights and safeguards for marginalized communities. Ambedkar was a champion of the Dalit cause,advocating for their rights and their inclusion in all aspects of society.He converted to Buddhism to escape the shackles of caste and inspired many others to do the same.His legacy lives on through his writings and his tireless efforts to create a more just and equitable society for all,making him an enduring icon of social reform and justice in India.

Biography

  • He dedicated his life to fighting for the rights and upliftment of Dalits (formerly known as untouchables) and other marginalized communities.
  • Despite facing caste-based discrimination,Ambedkar pursued higher education and earned multiple degrees,including a D.Sc. in Economics from the London School of Economics and a Ph.D. in Jurisprudence from the University of Columbia.
  • He was a key figure in the Indian independence movement and later became the chairman of the drafting committee for the Indian Constitution.
  • Ambedkar played a pivotal role in framing India's Constitution,ensuring fundamental rights and social justice for all citizens.
  • He championed the cause of Dalits by advocating for reservations in education and employment and working to eradicate untouchability.
  • Ambedkar converted to Buddhism in 1956,along with thousands of his followers,as a means of rejecting the caste system and discrimination.
  • He authored several influential books,including "Annihilation of Caste" and "The Problem of the Rupee:Its Origin and Its Solution."
  • B.R. Ambedkar's contributions to social justice,equality,and the Constitution continue to inspire and shape modern India.He is often referred to as the "Father of the Indian Constitution."
